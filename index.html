Why Savery: The Ethical, Moral, and Historical Justifications

The literary works of Frederick Douglass, Harriet Jacobs, and David Walker, really highlight issues in the system of slavery that existed in the United States, until it was outlawed in 1865, that make one question how others ever believed slavery was morally and ethically right...

Frederick Douglass's A Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass

Frederick Douglass's slave narrative, A Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass, shows the grotesque and brutal mindset of slave owners as well as the use of religion to support a profitable lifestyle...

Harriet Jacobs's Incidents in the Life of a Slave Girl

In her narrative, Incidents in the Life of a Slave Girl, Harriet Jacobs expresses some of the same sentiments towards religion and how it contributes to the institution of slavery...

Walker's Appeal & Douglass's Fourth of July Speech

Walker, in his appeal, approaches the same ideas from a different direction. In trying to prove to white people the atrocities they have committed, he related his reasoning back to stories in the Bible and historical evidence...

Justification Through Religion

While many arguments were made by those in slave-owning states to justify the practice of owning another human being like chattel, one of the most influential to the people of the antebellum South is the religious justifications...

Justification Through Historical Basis and Precedent

The other main support to the proslavery argument is the use of historical figures and precedents. Many times, this includes refences to the Greek Helots, the Roman slaves, and biblical references to the Israelites...

Conclusion

Centuries have passed and yet our country has not repaired the damage left by slavery practices. Most people will probably blow it off and say, "I have nothing to do with that. It's not my problem..."
